Policy Framework

Our Commitment to Fair Governance

The policies of the University of Mindanao are designed to ensure a safe, equitable, and productive learning environment. These policies are developed through consultative processes involving all stakeholders.

Academic Standards

Academic Policies

üìã Admission & Enrollment Policy ‚ñæ

The University of Mindanao accepts applications from all qualified students regardless of gender, religion, or socioeconomic status. Admission requirements vary by program level:

  • Undergraduate: High school diploma, entrance examination, interview
  • Graduate: Bachelor's degree, entrance examination, letter of intent
  • Transferees: Transcript of records, certificate of honorable dismissal
üìù Grading & Assessment Policy ‚ñæ

UM uses a numerical grading system on a scale of 1.0 to 5.0, with 1.0 being the highest. The passing grade for all undergraduate programs is 3.0, equivalent to 75%. Grades are computed based on class participation, written examinations, projects, and other assessments as specified in each course syllabus.

üéì Graduation Requirements ‚ñæ

To be eligible for graduation, students must complete all required units in their program with a GPA of 3.0 or better, have no outstanding financial obligations to the university, complete all required practicum/OJT hours, and pass the comprehensive examination where applicable.

üî¨ Research & Intellectual Property Policy ‚ñæ

All research conducted under the auspices of the University of Mindanao is subject to ethical review. Intellectual property created by faculty or students using university resources is governed by UM's Intellectual Property Policy, which provides for equitable sharing of benefits.

üìö Academic Integrity & Plagiarism Policy ‚ñæ

The University of Mindanao upholds the highest standards of academic honesty. Plagiarism, cheating, fabrication, and other forms of academic dishonesty are strictly prohibited. Violations are subject to disciplinary action ranging from a failing grade on the assignment to expulsion, depending on the severity of the offense.

Employment & Tenure

Faculty members are hired based on academic qualifications, professional experience, and demonstrated commitment to student learning. Tenure is granted after a probationary period and review of teaching performance, research output, and community service.
